A Commander Format

A little spinCommander on Wheels

Magic: Commander is already interesting enough. Let's shake it up anyway. Commander on Wheels is a format with this in mind; before a single card hits the table, a wheel is spun, and the result changes what your deck can be. A restriction might vanish. A bad card might suddenly be mandatory. Who comes out on top in a carousel?

Built on the standard Commander Bracket 3 ruleset, the rules keep everything you know the same, then layers on a live tournament structure, a deckbuilding twist, and a second chance for the defeated. It rewards foresight, adaptability, good play and great counterplay. But mostly, it's just for fun.

How It Works

Four cards, drawn in order

The game plays the same way each time: first the wheel is spun and you build a deck around its ruleset. Everyone brings their best to the table and round 1 begins. If you're eliminated, you'll get your get back.

I

The Fool

Four players per pod. Each deck runs ninety-nine cards plus one commander, built to the standard Commander Bracket 3 ruleset.

Bring friends, enemies, or strangers- the wheel only adds to the fun.

II

Wheel of Fortune

Before the first round of the event- The Great Wheel is spun. Its result is a specific, named exception to Commander rules, and it holds for the entire event.

An exception might restrict a choice you'd normally have, or unlock one you wouldn't- you might even have to spin an additional wheel...

III

The Tower

Magic on Wheels is played as a tournament, not a single sit-down. The first player to take two games takes the full event.

This game might be about luck, but it's about consistency and strategy all the same.

IV

Judgement

The players eliminated in each game aren't done for the match. Between rounds, they're given time to revise their decklists, which means more time if you're out sooner.

Getting knocked out early isn't all bad when it's a chance to counteract (or steal) your opponent's ideas.
